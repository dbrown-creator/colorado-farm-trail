# Monetization

> Status: **strategy notes, nothing built.** Captured early so the *how* is
> intentional. This is a trust-based, farmer-supporting brand — so the guiding rule
> comes first.

## The golden rule

**Monetize the value created _around_ the map (traffic, audience, experiences) —
never the neutrality of the map itself.** The moment pins are ranked by who paid, we
lose both the users and the farmers. Keep the core map free to farms and unbiased for
users; sell enhancements, experiences, and third-party access.

---

## A. Near-term, low-lift (once there's real traffic)

- **Featured listings (freemium).** Every farm stays on the map free. Farms *opt* to
  pay for an *enhanced* listing — extra photos, "what's in season now," a highlighted
  pin, top-of-category placement in their local area. Selling presentation, not
  position on the core map.
- **Tip jar / supporter membership.** A "Buy me a coffee" / Patreon-style tier
  ("Trail Supporter" — sticker + early access). Small money, but it *proves people
  value it* — useful evidence for bigger asks.
- **Merch + the physical Passport.** Hats, stickers, totes — and a printed **Passport
  booklet** ($15–25) people buy, get stamped at farms, and redeem for a completion
  prize. Profitable for rail-trail / brewery-passport programs, and doubles as free
  marketing (people wear/carry the brand). Ties directly to the gamification plan
  (see [`gamification.md`](gamification.md)).

## B. Mission-aligned & scalable (the real revenue)

- **A title sponsor.** One aligned regional brand sponsors the trail or the Passport —
  a farm credit union, grocery co-op, ag insurer, or outdoor/lifestyle brand. Cleanest
  big-check path *because* a mission-fit sponsor reinforces trust instead of eroding
  it. The GA4 traffic + share data (see [`ANALYTICS.md`](ANALYTICS.md)) is the pitch.
- **Tourism / agritourism partnerships.** The Colorado Tourism Office and regional
  destination-marketing orgs pay to promote farm visits and rural weekend trips —
  exactly what the map drives. Pitch a co-branded regional trail.
- **Grants.** As a local-food-systems + rural-economy project: USDA Local Food
  Promotion, state ag department, and food-systems foundations. Usually requires a
  nonprofit or a **fiscal sponsor** — see the structure note below.

## C. Commerce & long-game

- **Booking / affiliate cut.** Where farms sell *experiences* — U-pick reservations,
  winery tastings, farm dinners, CSA sign-ups — take a referral/affiliate fee for
  driving the booking. Wineries (~8) and agritourism (~10) are the highest-value.
  "OpenTable for farm experiences."
- **Events.** Host farm crawls / harvest festivals → ticket sales, vendor fees, event
  sponsors.
- **Aggregated interest data.** Anonymized, privacy-safe insight — which counties,
  products, and farms draw the most interest — is valuable to farms, extension
  offices, and tourism boards. Sell reports, never sell people.
- **License the playbook.** The scalable asset: the data pipeline + map + Passport +
  analytics is a repeatable template. Once it works in Colorado, license/franchise
  "Farm Trail" to other states. The tech and process are the product.

---

## What to avoid

- **Pay-to-rank pins** or paywalling farms out of the core map — kills trust both ways.
- **Charging small farms to exist** on the map — they're the beneficiaries. Monetize
  *enhancements* and *third parties*, not farmers' access.
- **Generic display ads** — cheap money that cheapens the brand fast.

## Structural decision (make it early-ish)

Monetization ceiling depends on entity type:

| Entity | Unlocks | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|
| **LLC** | Sponsorships, merch, affiliate, licensing | Not grant-eligible |
| **Nonprofit / fiscal sponsorship** | Grants + tourism/gov partnerships | More overhead |
| **Hybrid** (nonprofit program + for-profit licensing arm) | Both | Most complex; common for mission-with-scale projects |

Not tonight's decision — but it's the fork that determines whether grant and
tourism-board money is on the table.

---

## Suggested sequence

1. **Prove traffic** (already instrumented via GA4).
2. Land **one mission-fit title sponsor** + sell the **printed Passport**.
3. Layer in **tourism / grant** money once the check-in data shows real farm-visit
   impact.

The gamified **Passport** is the linchpin — both the engagement engine *and* the
impact proof every sponsor and grant will want to see.
