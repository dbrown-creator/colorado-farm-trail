# Branding

## The brand

**Name:** Colorado Farm Trail
**Tagline (primary):** *"Find your next farm stop."*
**Game hook / sub-brand:** **The Farm Trail Passport** — *"Collect Colorado, one farm at a time."*

**One-line description:**
> Colorado Farm Trail is a free road-trip game. Pull it up on your phone, drive the
> state, and check in at farms, markets, and U-picks along the way — while a live
> counter tracks the dollars the community is driving to local growers.

---

## Positioning (the category-definition exercise)

| Dimension | Answer |
|---|---|
| **Who it's for** | Coloradans (and visitors) who want fresh local food and love a weekend farm outing / in-state road trip |
| **Job it does** | "When I want farm-fresh food or a day trip, show me the nearby spots and get me there — fast, on my phone" |
| **Category** | A free community **map + guide** to Colorado's local food (farms, markets, U-picks, stands, wineries) |
| **What it's NOT** | Not official/government, not a marketplace, not delivery — pure *discovery* |
| **Personality** | Warm, generous, community-first, a little adventurous |
| **Rally cry** | "Get more Coloradans eating local this summer" |
| **Brand promise** | The easiest way to find farm-fresh Colorado |

The **road-trip / discovery** angle is the chosen north star: the mental image is
someone pulling the map up on their phone as they head out for an in-state weekend,
finding farm stops along the route.

---

## Naming decision

Chosen: **Colorado Farm Trail** — leans into discovery, agritourism, and the
road-trip/journey feel. Directly supports the "collect the state" gamification idea.

### Alternatives considered
| Name | Vibe | Why not (for now) |
|---|---|---|
| **Homegrown Colorado** | Warm, inclusive, movement-y | Great, but implies a broader community brand there's no vision for yet — risked feeling flat |
| **Fresh Colorado** | Clean, modern, utility | Generic; clean handles/domains hard to get |
| **Field & Fork Colorado** | Foodie, editorial, premium | More boutique than the mass road-trip audience wants |

### Explicitly avoided
- **"Colorado Proud"** and close variants — that's the State of Colorado's official
  program (and the source of the underlying directory data). The brand must read as
  clearly **independent**. Data is credited as a source, nothing more.

---

## Voice & tone

- Warm, encouraging, a little playful. Emoji welcome (🌽 🚜 🍑 🥕 🌻).
- Talks like a friend sharing a good tip, not an institution.
- Always ends on the ask: *eat local, and pass it on.*

## Visual direction (starter, not locked)

- **Palette:** field/earth greens as primary, warm harvest accents (peach/gold).
  The landing page currently uses green `#3f7d3a` (light) / `#7bb56f` (dark).
- **Feel:** clean, outdoorsy, mobile-first. Think trail signage meets farmstand.
