# Rollout — 2026 Season

## The multi-season frame

- **This season (2026):** capture traffic → build a user base → stand up the
  **no-code digital check-in** → collect farm-visit + dollar impact data. All free.
- **Off-season:** use that data to line up a title sponsor + design the printed
  Passport and prize/redemption mechanics.
- **Next season (2027):** launch the **printed Passport program** with sponsor
  backing, priced against real, proven demand.

The mechanism monetized next year is *validated this year at zero cost.*

## Timing reality

Launched mid-July. Colorado's season peaks **now through October** (peaches → sweet
corn → apples/pumpkins). This is **launch fast and ride the peak**, not a slow build —
roughly a **12–14 week window**. The two highest-leverage moves (farms sharing it +
posting to the harvest calendar) are both strongest *right now*.

## Week-by-week

### Week 1 — Soft launch
- [ ] Finish Phase 0: site live on GitHub Pages, GA4 confirmed in **Realtime**.
- [ ] Share with a **tiny circle first** (5–10 friends/family) — catch broken links,
      confirm it saves to their phones, collect first reactions.
- [ ] Stand up the **digital check-in form** (no-code Google Form) so impact data
      accrues from day one, even quietly. See [`gamification.md`](gamification.md).

### Weeks 2–3 — Seed with the warm network
- [ ] Push the ready-made posts (text, email, IG, FB) from [`MARKETING.md`](MARKETING.md).
- [ ] Goal: **first 50–100 users** + a couple quotable testimonials.
- [ ] Always include the ask: *"if it's useful, send it to one friend."*

### Weeks 3–6 — Enlist the farms (highest-leverage move)
This is the one that actually scales, and it's win-win.
- [ ] DM/email the mapped farms + market managers: *"I built a free map that sends
      customers your way — here's your pin. Mind sharing it with your followers?"*
- [ ] 163 businesses, each with an audience — even 25–30 sharing = reach you can't buy.
- [ ] **QR codes at farmers markets** (flyer copy in `MARKETING.md`); ask market
      managers to display them.

### Weeks 4–8 — Go where the "where do I get…" questions live
- [ ] **Local Facebook groups & Nextdoor** (neighborhood, CO foodie, gardening,
      homesteading) — answer "best local peaches?" threads *with the map*.
- [ ] **Reddit** (r/Colorado, r/Denver, r/ColoradoSprings) — check each sub's
      self-promo rules first.
- [ ] **Micro-influencers:** small CO food / mom / outdoor-lifestyle accounts.

### Mid-season — Press & partners (ride the harvest hooks)
- [ ] Pitch local media "things to do this summer/fall" desks (5280, Denver Post,
      local TV) + food bloggers.
- [ ] Time posts to **what's ripe**: Palisade peaches (Aug), sweet corn (Aug),
      apples/pumpkins (Sept–Oct). Seasonality *is* the content calendar.

### All season — Sustain & measure
- [ ] Light weekly rhythm: a "farm of the week" / "what's in season now" post that
      nudges people back and prompts check-ins.
- [ ] Watch GA4 weekly; **double down on whichever channel wins** (UTM tags tell you),
      drop what doesn't. See [`ANALYTICS.md`](ANALYTICS.md).

## The two moves that punch above everything

1. **Get the farms themselves to distribute it** — right audience, zero cost, win-win.
2. **Time posts to the harvest** — each ripening crop is a fresh reason to re-share.

Both are free, and both are strongest right now.

## Season success markers

Track these — the last one is both distribution *and* the sponsor-pitch proof:

- Total users (GA4)
- `open_map` conversions (did they actually open the map)
- Check-ins logged
- Dollars reported to farmers
- **# of farms who shared it**
