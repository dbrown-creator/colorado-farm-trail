Mission
Our mission is to celebrate and elevate the spirit of the Vail Valley by bringing together farmers, artisans, chefs, and our community to share the best of local food, craft, and culture. Through the Vail Farmers’ Market & Art Show, The Thursday Meadow Market, the Kris Kringle Holiday Market, and seasonal Farm-to-Table dinners, we foster meaningful connections between visitors, locals, and the talented makers who shape our mountain lifestyle.

Vision
We envision Vail as a year-round destination where food, art, and community thrive in every season, from summer markets bursting with Colorado produce and handcrafted goods to winter streets glowing with holiday charm, creating unforgettable experiences that support small businesses, nourish our region, and celebrate the beauty and creativity of mountain life.

Parking & Getting to the Vail Farmers’ Market

Vail Village is designed for pedestrians, so parking in the immediate area is limited. We recommend planning ahead and using Vail’s convenient parking structures and free transportation system.

Where to Park

The easiest parking options for the market are:

  • Vail Village Parking Structure closest access to the market

  • Lionshead Parking Structure great for overflow with quick bus access

  • Red Sandstone Parking Garage additional parking with free bus service

All parking structures connect to the free Town of Vail bus system, which runs frequently between Lionshead and Vail Village.

Free & Alternative Options

  • Donovan Park free daytime parking (when available)

  • Ford Park event and overflow parking

Getting Around

Once parked, getting to the market is easy:

  • Take the free bus into Vail Village

  • Walk or bike along Vail’s scenic paths

  • Use your hotel shuttle if you’re staying locally

Local Tips

  • Arrive early for the best parking (especially Sundays)

  • Parking garages can fill quickly during peak hours

  • If you’re staying in Vail, leave your car and walk or take the bus

  • Locals are encouraged to bike or ride the bus

Oversized Vehicles

Oversized vehicles and trailers are not permitted in the village core and must park in designated areas along the North Frontage Road in West Vail.
